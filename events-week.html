Overview

The Christian Union exists to share the Good News of Jesus Christ with students at LSE.

Every year, we run our Events Week in Lent term to give people a chance to hear the Gospel on campus, with lunchtime events (with free lunch!) every day on campus plus London-wide events every evening at All Souls, Langham Place.

This year's Events Week on the theme "Rethink" runs from Monday 26th to Friday 30th January 2015.

Monday

Our first event, "Rethink the Bible: More than myth?", runs on Monday 26th January 2015 at 1pm in KSW 1.04 ([map]) with a chance to discover whether the Bible is truly reliable over a free lunch.

In the evening, join us at All Souls, Langham Place (5 minutes from Oxford Circus tube) from 7pm for a 60s festival-themed evening with live music, relaxed atmosphere and a short talk on the theme "Dancing in the Street: the God who brings us joy". For more details, see the Facebook event.

Tuesday

On Tuesday, join us for "Rethink life's big questions", offering a great chance to quiz Christians about what they believe. You'll find us in KSW 1.04 ([map]) at 4pm.

In the evening from 7pm, join us at "You Really Got Me: the God who knows us" at All Souls with music, food, drink and chat.

Wednesday

On Wednesday, join us in STC S78 ([map]) at 1pm to grab a free lunch and hear about suffering from a Christian perspective in "Rethink suffering: does God care?".

In the evening from 7pm, join us for another 60s festival themed event at All Souls on the theme "I Can't Stop Loving You: the God everyone needs" with food, drink, music and a chance to ask questions.

Thursday

On Thursday, we'll be running our event "Rethink success: what is true achievement?" in KSW 1.04 ([map]) at 1pm with a free lunch and short talk.

The last evening event of the week starts at All Souls at 7pm on the theme "Stand By Me: the God who Loves us".

Friday

We'll finish up the week with a final lunchtime event on the topic "Rethink acceptance: will God welcome me?" in KSW 1.04 ([map]) at 1pm. Join us for free lunch, a brief talk and the chance to ask questions.
